    • My Process

    Product Design is the entire process of creating usable products and experiences, starting by defining real human problems and collaboratively working toward successful and measurable solutions. I have used the double diamond as a guide in my work for as long as I can remember. The concept is to do the right thing, the right way. Read on to see how I typically apply it.

    üîç Discover

    This is about building empathy and understanding all the human problems we can solve that will help us achieve our goal. I typically do this by:

    • Revisiting previous research
    • Internal interviews
    • Viewing user recordings
    • Analysing quantitaitve user behaviour
    • Interviewing users
    • User surveys
    • Competitive & comparitve research

    üèπ Define

    Now we have collected all the interesting data, I work closely to synthesise our findings to define a specific user problem that has measurable success toward our goal. I typically do this by:

    • Workshopping / Facilitation
    • Impact / Effort
    • Problem statement (e.g. HMW)
    • Journey Mapping
    • Problem statements
    • Personas
    • Opportunity sizing

    üå™ Ideate

    Great! We have a problem to solve. Here is the fun bit where we go wide on all the ways we can solve that problem - Small and large. Our goal is to satisfy our user and meet their needs. I typically do this by:

    • Brainstorming / Facilitation
    • Design Sprints
    • Sketching
    • Card sorting & Tree testing
    • Prototyping (multi-fi)

    üõ† Test

    We're so far confident in the soluton/s we're working on. Our goal is to test it, iterate on it, challenge our assumptions and learn/fail fast. I build confidence in our idea, or direction by:

    • Usability testing
    • Guerilla testing
    • A/B testing
    • Fake Doors
